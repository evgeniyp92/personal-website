A small thing, kept on the inside cover of every notebook I own. I copy it over when I start a new one. It is not a system, which is the first reason I trust it.

## The questions

1. **What am I actually doing this week?** — not the calendar, the verb.
2. **What did I think was true on Monday that I don't think is true now?** — the week should move me, or what was it for.
3. **What would I be embarrassed to still be working on in six weeks?** — a gentle, private deadline.

## Why three

Three is enough to triangulate and not enough to avoid. Two feels like a pair of arguments; four feels like a meeting. Three is a stool: sit on it, it holds. Pull one leg, it falls.

I answer them in fountain pen, in the margin, on Friday afternoon. The answers are for no one. I reread them once, on the following Friday, to see which was the lie and which was the weather.
